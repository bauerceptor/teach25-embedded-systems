# Chapter 4: Electronics for IoT

## Overview
This chapter focuses on electronic components and circuits specifically designed for Internet of Things applications, including sensors, actuators, communication modules, and power management systems.

## Learning Objectives
- Understand IoT-specific electronic components and their applications
- Learn about sensor interfacing and signal conditioning
- Explore wireless communication modules and protocols
- Design power management circuits for battery-operated devices
- Integrate electronic components into complete IoT systems

## Key Topics Covered
- IoT system architecture and electronic components
- Sensor types and interfacing (temperature, humidity, motion, light)
- Actuator control (motors, LEDs, relays, servos)
- Analog-to-digital and digital-to-analog conversion
- Signal conditioning and amplification circuits
- Wireless communication modules (WiFi, Bluetooth, LoRa)
- Power management and battery systems
- Low-power design techniques
- PCB design considerations for IoT devices
- Environmental considerations and protection circuits

## Prerequisites
- Fundamental Electronics I (Chapter 3)
- Basic understanding of digital systems
- Knowledge of microcontroller concepts

## Laboratory Activities
- Sensor calibration and interfacing experiments
- Wireless communication module testing
- Power consumption measurement and optimization
- IoT device prototyping and testing
- PCB design and assembly projects
