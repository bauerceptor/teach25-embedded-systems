## Fill-in-the-Blank Questions

### Question 1
Electricity is a <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'secondary', 'exp1')">____</span> form of energy because it must be produced from other energy sources.

<div id="exp1" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Electricity is called a secondary form of energy because it cannot be found naturally in a usable form. It must be generated by converting primary energy sources like kinetic energy (water), thermal energy (coal), or solar energy into electrical energy.
</div>

### Question 2
The unit of electrical resistance is <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'ohm', 'exp2')">____</span>, represented by the Greek letter omega (Ω).

<div id="exp2" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> The ohm is named after German physicist Georg Simon Ohm who formulated Ohm's Law. One ohm is the resistance that allows one ampere of current to flow when one volt is applied across it.
</div>

### Question 3
In a series circuit, the <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'current', 'exp3')">____</span> remains the same through all components.

<div id="exp3" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> In series circuits, there is only one path for current to flow, so the same amount of current must pass through each component. However, voltage divides among the components based on their resistance values.
</div>

### Question 4
The opposition to current flow in a circuit increases when temperature <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'increases', 'exp4')">____</span> in conductors.

<div id="exp4" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> In conductors, higher temperature causes atoms to vibrate more, creating more obstacles for electron flow. This increases resistance, which is why electrical equipment can become less efficient when overheated.
</div>

### Question 5
AC voltage can be easily transformed using <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'transformers', 'exp5')">____</span>, making it suitable for long-distance transmission.

<div id="exp5" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Transformers work on electromagnetic induction principle and can only operate with changing magnetic fields produced by AC. They allow voltage to be stepped up for efficient transmission and stepped down for safe consumer use.
</div>

### Question 6
In parallel circuits, the <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'voltage', 'exp6')">____</span> across all branches remains the same.

<div id="exp6" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Parallel circuits have all components connected to the same two points, so they all experience the same potential difference (voltage). Current divides among the branches based on their resistance values.
</div>

### Question 7
The <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'electrolyte', 'exp7')">____</span> in a battery provides a medium for ion movement between electrodes.

<div id="exp7" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Electrolyte is a chemical solution that allows positive and negative ions to move between anode and cathode, completing the internal circuit while electrons flow externally through the connected device.
</div>

### Question 8
Semiconductors become <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'better conductors', 'exp8')">____</span> when temperature increases.

<div id="exp8" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Unlike metals, semiconductors have more electrons gaining energy to jump to the conduction band at higher temperatures. This increases the number of charge carriers, improving conductivity - opposite to conductor behavior.
</div>

### Question 9
The <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'specific gravity', 'exp9')">____</span> test is the most accurate method to determine battery state of charge.

<div id="exp9" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Specific gravity measures the concentration of acid in battery electrolyte. Higher specific gravity indicates stronger acid and better charge state. A hydrometer is used to perform this test.
</div>

### Question 10
Power transmission lines use <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'high voltage', 'exp10')">____</span> to reduce current and minimize power losses.

<div id="exp10" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Using P = VI, the same power can be transmitted with high voltage and low current. Since power loss follows P = I²R, lower current significantly reduces losses in transmission lines over long distances.
</div>

### Question 11
The <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'anode', 'exp11')">____</span> is the negative electrode in a battery that releases electrons during discharge.

<div id="exp11" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> The anode undergoes oxidation reaction during discharge, losing electrons which flow through the external circuit to the cathode. This electron flow constitutes the electric current we use.
</div>

### Question 12
A <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'galvanometer', 'exp12')">____</span> is the basic instrument used in both ammeters and voltmeters.

<div id="exp12" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> A galvanometer detects and measures small electric currents. Ammeters use it with low resistance shunts, while voltmeters use it with high resistance multipliers to measure current and voltage respectively.
</div>

### Question 13
Lithium-ion batteries have the best <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'energy to weight ratio', 'exp13')">____</span> among common battery types.

<div id="exp13" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Lithium is the lightest metal and has high electrochemical potential, allowing lithium-ion batteries to store more energy per unit weight compared to other battery technologies, making them ideal for portable devices.
</div>

### Question 14
The <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'load test', 'exp14')">____</span> simulates actual operating conditions to check battery performance under stress.

<div id="exp14" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Load testing applies a controlled electrical load to the battery while monitoring voltage drop. This reveals the battery's ability to deliver current under actual operating conditions, which simple voltage measurement cannot determine.
</div>

### Question 15
In the electrical grid, <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'substations', 'exp15')">____</span> are used to change voltage levels for different parts of the distribution system.

<div id="exp15" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Substations contain transformers that step voltage up or down as needed. They convert high transmission voltage to medium distribution voltage and finally to low consumer voltage at different stages of the electrical grid.
</div>

### Question 16
The phenomenon where voltage decreases due to internal resistance when current flows is called <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'voltage drop', 'exp16')">____</span>.

<div id="exp16" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> All real voltage sources have internal resistance. When current flows, some voltage is "lost" across this internal resistance (V = I × R_internal), making the terminal voltage lower than the EMF.
</div>

### Question 17
A <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'hydrometer', 'exp17')">____</span> is used to measure the specific gravity of battery electrolyte.

<div id="exp17" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> A hydrometer measures the density of liquids relative to water. In batteries, it determines acid concentration in the electrolyte, which directly relates to the battery's state of charge.
</div>

### Question 18
The process of adding impurities to semiconductors to control their conductivity is called <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'doping', 'exp18')">____</span>.

<div id="exp18" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Doping involves adding small amounts of specific elements to pure semiconductors. This creates additional charge carriers (electrons or holes) and allows precise control of electrical properties for electronic devices.
</div>

### Question 19
Battery capacity is measured in <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'ampere-hours', 'exp19')">____</span>, indicating how much current it can supply over time.

<div id="exp19" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> Ampere-hours (Ah) represents the total charge a battery can deliver. For example, a 10 Ah battery can theoretically supply 1 ampere for 10 hours or 2 amperes for 5 hours.
</div>

### Question 20
The <span class="blank" onclick="revealAnswer(this, 'grid', 'exp20')">____</span> is the interconnected network that delivers electricity from power plants to consumers.

<div id="exp20" class="explanation">
<strong>Explanation:</strong> The electrical grid is a complex network of power plants, transmission lines, substations, transformers, and distribution systems that work together to provide reliable electricity supply across large geographic areas.
</div>
