## Learning Objectives Summary

By completing these interactive exercises, students will have reinforced their understanding of:

1. **Network Classification:** Different types of networks (PAN, LAN, MAN, WAN) and their characteristics
2. **Network Devices:** Functions of hubs, switches, routers, and their operational layers
3. **Transmission Media:** Cable types, their properties, and appropriate applications
4. **Network Topologies:** Star, bus, ring, mesh topologies and their trade-offs
5. **OSI Model:** Seven layers and their specific functions in network communication
6. **TCP/IP Protocols:** Four-layer model and key protocols (TCP, UDP, IP)
7. **IP Addressing:** IPv4/IPv6 addressing, subnetting, and address management
8. **Network Testing:** Tools and techniques for troubleshooting connectivity
9. **Wireless Networking:** WiFi standards, security, and challenges
10. **Network Security:** Fundamental security concepts and protection methods
11. **IoT Networking:** Special requirements and technologies for IoT applications
12. **Performance Optimization:** Bandwidth, latency, QoS, and network optimization
13. **Emerging Technologies:** SDN, NFV, 5G, and future networking trends

These concepts form the foundation for understanding how modern networks support IoT systems, cloud computing, and distributed applications in today's interconnected world.
